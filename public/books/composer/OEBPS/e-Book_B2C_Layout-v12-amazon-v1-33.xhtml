31 – Slave To The Rhythm – Day 2 – Understanding simple rhythm

– Half notes (clap on beats 1 & 3).

– Quarter notes (clap on beats 1, 2, 3, 4).

Repeat this exercise but instead of clapping, play the root note you chose on Day One. Play it anywhere over the piano, but always keep the rhythm and pulse intact.

Whenever you try an exercise in this book, repeat it multiple times, only calling it a day when you can complete it without mistakes. It’s easy to skip over this advice, but repetition is the fastest way to ingrain new concepts and develop the muscle memory that will stand you in good stead.

Turn to your worksheet. It’s time to create an interesting rhythmic phrase.

Rules

Some simple rules will help you get to grips with the possibilities:

– Whole notes = Four beats. They can only be placed on beat one of a four beat bar, because whole notes last for all four beats of the bar.

– Half notes = Two beats. They can only be placed on beats one, two or three.

– Quarter notes = One beat. They can be placed on any beat.
