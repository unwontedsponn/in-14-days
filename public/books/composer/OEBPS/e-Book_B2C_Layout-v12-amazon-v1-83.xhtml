You Met Your Match

Creating the B section of your composition in a minor key

Keys come in pairs

You’ve played a lot already in your major key, so you’re probably feeling like a change. So you’re going to move into the relative minor key. Every major key has a relative, corresponding minor key, and it is really easy to work out what it is. The relative minor key is always the sixth note of the major key.

Example: The relative minor of the C major key is A minor
