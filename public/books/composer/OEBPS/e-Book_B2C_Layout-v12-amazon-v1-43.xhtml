41 – Ground Control To Major Tom – Day 3 – Playing in a major key

Major keys that start on sharp notes

Remember enharmonic equivalents? That’s right, a note that shares the same pitch as another note, though it bears a different name e.g. Db and C#. All of the major keys that start on flat notes can also be written enharmonically, however it is only common to see Db and Gb major written in their enharmonic form.

Db major: Db Eb F Gb Ab Bb C Db

C# major: C# D# E# F# G# A# B# C#

Did you notice how E# and B# are white notes in C# major?

Gb major: Gb Ab Bb Cb Db Eb F Gb

F# major: F# G# A# B C# D# E# F#

Did you notice how Cb is a white note in Gb major? And how E# and B# are white notes in F# major?
