19 – Ebony & Ivory – Day 1 – Exploring the notes of the piano

C notes

At first glance the keys of the piano look complex, but chances are you can already see a pattern emerging. Black keys are in groups of twos and threes. You can use these groups as signposts, to navigate your way to any other note with ease. I’ll demonstrate with the note C. C’s are always located to the left of black key pairs. How many C’s are there on your piano? Take a moment to work it out.

Exploring the notes of the piano

What’s the difference between all of the notes we call C? The answer is pitch. Some sound high, some sound low. Others sound somewhere in between. The C in the middle of the piano is called middle C. The distance between a C and the next C to its left or right is called an octave (8ve).
