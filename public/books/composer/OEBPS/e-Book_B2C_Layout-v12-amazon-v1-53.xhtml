Three common chords

You’ve probably already noticed how some groups of notes sound great together, while others just don’t. Here’s why... There are relationships between notes. Some notes are well-matched, some are unstable. The quicker you get to know which ones make beautiful music, you’ll do just that. All music, from nursery rhymes to complex jazz, can be boiled down to three common groups of notes we call chords. To be even more precise they are triad chords, reflecting the fact they are made up of three notes.

Major chords: Sound happy.

Minor chords: Sad.

Diminished chords: Dark and edgy.

All that’s needed to play these chords is a simple formula.
