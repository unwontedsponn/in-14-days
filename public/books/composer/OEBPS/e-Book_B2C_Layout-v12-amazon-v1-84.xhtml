82 – You Met Your Match – Day 8 – Creating the B section of your composition in a minor key

The notes of the relative minor key will be the same as the notes of its major pair. The only difference is that you start in a different place.

Example: A minor starts in a different place to C major but has the same notes

C major key

R T T S T T T S

C D E F G A B C

A minor key

R T S T T S T T

A B C D E F G A

This makes the formula for the notes of minor keys: RTSTTSTT

Minor scale finger patterns

The easiest way to play minor scales is of course with finger patterns.

Example: A minor RH (one octave)

R T S T T S T T

A B C D E F G A

1 2 3 1 2 3 4 5
